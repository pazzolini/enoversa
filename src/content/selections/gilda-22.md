### The Atlantic Narrative

To drink Tiago Teles is to drink the wind of the Atlantic. There is no hiding the proximity to the ocean in this bottle. 

**Gilda** has always been a wine of "thirst" (*vinho de sede*), but the 2022 vintage brings a nervous energy that is particularly compelling. It is not a wine that sits politely in the glass; it vibrates. It is a reaction against the heavy, extracted wines that dominated the region for decades—a return to the true nature of the cool coastal climate.

#### The Profile
Constructed largely from Castelão and Merlot, it defies the heavy, tannic expectations often associated with Bairrada. Teles treats the grapes with a gentle hand—infusion rather than extraction.

* **Nose:** Crunchy wild berries, wet pavement (petrichor), and a distinct note of sea spray. There is a slight reduction upon opening that blows off to reveal bright red fruit.
* **Palate:** Electric acidity. It cuts through the humidity of a warm afternoon. The fruit is tart—cranberry and pomegranate—but the finish is entirely mineral and saline.

#### The Landscape
This wine speaks of the cool, damp mornings of the region. It doesn't try to be a Douro powerhouse or an Alentejo fruit bomb. It is honest to its cool-climate roots. It asks for food—perhaps a simple grilled sardine or a pork sandwich (bifana)—but it stands alone as a piece of liquid architecture.

**Drinkability:** High.  
**Mood:** Restless energy.
